PhonePe 2.0 - BTS

Verdict [Time Saver]

PhonePe’s sudden UI revamp faced backlash as users, especially merchants, struggled with the abrupt change. Disrupting muscle memory risks frustration, making gradual updates preferable.

The old design, though cluttered, was familiar, while the new bento grid layout feels modern and structured. Multi-language support has improved, but dark mode needs accessibility refinements.

UPI apps share similar layouts due to NPCI guidelines and Jakob’s Law, ensuring familiarity and fair competition. Users rarely switch apps due to habit and trust. PhonePe’s redesign aligns with competitors but must balance innovation with user comfort.

After 2.0

This new revamp gives lot of trust and clarity in my opinion. First which I notice is the elegance which I get than the older design layout, and it is clean and neat.

In their old design, We could see the lot of subcategories upfront, But here in the revamp the categories and clubbed into minimal cards - which is called bento grids. Bento box is a Japanese tradition of packing food into box.

Depending on their priorities and the size that brings them the most revenue, they have maintained a variety of box sizes. They had distinct icons for each of the subcategories in the previous design, but now that they are claiming to be redesigning it for accessibility, they have included the features in the box itself.

This revamp also helps to have multiple language support with ease, For example we have gpay on the right and phonePe on left. If I change language it wraps text but the phonePe design is good for multiple languages and accessible for everyone. The information architecture of the cards are well structured, based on the features where they get good business. Now in the each box they have a cool illustration which is subtle and elegant to explain what the card contains.

Design for UPI Apps

If you can see all the UPI apps have very similar UI, One of the reasons from a pure play design stand point will be Jackob’s Law which states that the people who use a niche category app which serves almost same service may expect same service anyway

There is a catch again, These apps are structured and made with almost similar UI as a part of NPCI guidelines, Let me explain better. NPCI’s Volume cap guideline - OC97, Where they have constraint to limit the transactions to 30% around all apps. NPCI has analyzed the risks in the UPI
ecosystem. To address the risks and protect the UPI ecosystem they have rolled out these guidelines and you can wonder why we have so many apps for UPI with almost everything same layout

Lot of apps might have same features and functionality just because of this NPCI guidelines are meant to follow, they do roll out brand guidelines for their logo, so it doesn’t confuse people. In verdict designing for UPI apps also need to have same layout over all other apps and they follow due to this. Leave design, they should also features like UPI lite, Autopay and other features which they have.

Surprisingly users who use UPI apps don’t change the app they use, because they will find difficult using unfamiliar layouts and features. As we use it daily and we stick to a muscle memory, trust and ease. Users find their current UPI is sufficient enough to use and there is no alternative is significantly better. So PhonePe has tried to make a similar one with their competitors but yet simple layout and this is how the design is evolved.
