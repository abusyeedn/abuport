01 Introduction

People have little time to devote to their health in this fast-paced environment. In today's corporate culture, people order food from restaurants on a daily basis to save time, but they overlook an important aspect: their health. Yes, I'm referring to a user's calorie intake in their daily life. They are unaware of how many calories they are consuming or how it will impact them in the future. To address this issue, GreenBite was founded, and they offer a variety of organic pre-meal kits tailored to a user's specific nutrient needs, such as carbs, fiber, protein, etc.

About GreenBite

GreenBite is a fictional organic meal kit delivery service that focuses on providing customers with fresh, high-quality ingredients for easy and nutritious home-cooked meals. The primary goal is to promote a healthier lifestyle by providing a carefully curated selection of organic produce and meal kits, making it easier to prepare delicious, nutritious meals.

Statement

As I mentioned before, I am writing this for an event, so they have given me the challenge as

Your task is to design the landing page for "GreenBite," a fictional organic food delivery service. The service focuses on delivering fresh, organic produce and healthy meal kits to customers' doorsteps. Your design should reflect the brand's commitment to health, sustainability, and convenience. Design for both desktop and mobile version.

02 Goal

The goal is to create a landing page that caters to all types of users and makes them feel at ease when using Green Bite's services. There are objectives provided, such as

  • User Engagement: Create a visually appealing and intuitive interface that engages users from the first glance.
  • Brand Representation: Incorporate elements that represent health, organic produce, and sustainability.
  • Clear Call-to-Action (CTA): Design a prominent CTA that encourages users to sign up or explore products.

So I'll be designing GreenBite with all of the aforementioned considerations in mind. I'll walk you through it from inspiration to how I ended up creating this page.

03 The Approach

Design Process

  • Empathize
  • Define
  • Ideate
  • Prototype
  • Testing

Empathize

Although the empathize phase should be based on exhaustive testing by end users, I am using online sources to analyze insights from qualitative and quantitative user research. This is basically a collection of assumptions and considerations. I used the basic design thinking process, but in this case study, as I mentioned, I didn't research, but we were able to identify problems that will affect people.

Define

In this section, I defined a type of person; because this is a user-centric approach, it will undoubtedly help to create a better landing page. This can lead to more accessible and usable designs.

Type

  • Health Conscious
  • Corporate Trapped

Health Conscious

As the person will be more health conscious for themselves and for their family and have chances to make that as routine if the services are in daily plan, so will see this in a brief manner. I took one my friend for this persona achieving better understanding.

Corporate Trapped

These type of users have very hectic lifestyle and have very minimum time to have healthy food, Maybe GreenBite can help them to eat healthy organic food on daily basis according to their needs.

Ideate

I thoroughly read the pains and frustrations of the persona and came up with many ideas to solve and for designing this landing page. In terms of color schemes, I had a clear mental image that words like organic and sustainable could be associated with green or shades of green. I also have the required header menu from the given, as they specified it should be minimal.

Logo

Initially, I created a logo because it is everything that constitutes an overall structure and is a wordmark. I simplified it by using basic font settings that express green and bite visually, similar to "GreenBite".

Takeout box

I created a takeout box in Adobe Dimension and inserted the package design into the takeout box to make it more visually appealing. There are many templates on the internet, but I couldn't find the angle I was looking for, so I created it myself. The packet contains information such as the price, name of the product, and a nutrition table.

Initial Ideas

I created a hero section with an image that contains a lot of information at first glance, so that even new users can get an idea of the product or service they provide. I kept a headline to capture the attention of users, and it worked very well when tested, as you will see in the following topics. To highlight the primary CTA, I used the normal brand color. It is placed in the center of the landing page to encourage users to explore further and learn more about the different types or categories of products they offer.

Visual Appeal

As previously stated, I have included an image of a takeout meal as well as a customer review. This instills trust in users when they see the benefits of the product and decide to proceed without hesitation. Down the page, I added products that users can purchase if they are interested in the product. To capture their attention, I used phrases like "Top Seller" and "Value Pack" on specific products.

In addition, I mentioned the brand and their goal of eradicating fast food culture while empowering all users with nutritious food. So these elements are included to build trust with both new and existing users.

Components & Interaction

I also created components in the project for both primary and secondary CTAs, as well as for the website alone, to distinguish between pressed and hover states. There is another component used in the header menu with animated bars underline, this scroll down easily to the required area. In addition, the mobile version of the website includes a hamburger icon, which is used to group the header menu.

I've made the header interactive so that you can hover to see what you're hovering over, and it will scroll through the page based on the menu. nu for easier usability on small mobile phones.

Color & Typography

I used a dark shade of green and light green for better contrast. As usual I used greys for dividers and pressed animation with slight color change.

04 Testing

I always use Maze or Useberry to test my designs with real users; this will undoubtedly reveal potential changes that can be made as an iteration in design to improve the usability of the design. Instead we can evaluate the heuristics of the design with usability laws.

As it is a fictional, I gave a try to attention insight plugin for heatmaps and it has given lot of insights.

  • Headline - 34.5%
  • Image - 25.6%
  • Primary CTA - 4.8%
  • Logo - 2.8%
  • Secondary CTA - 1.4%

So this is AI-generated, but it has a good algorithm that clearly analyzes the actual website. I received an optimal score of 66, which is ideal for a landing page. In this case, the headline is longer than average and provides a clear description of the product. In addition the image has around 25% of coverage and it is highlighted in the review section which will build trust to the user
